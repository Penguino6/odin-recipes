Roti

Description

Roti is a traditional Indian flatbread that is usually eaten with curries. It holds a
similar place as tortillas in hispanic/south american cultures. Unlike naan, which
is more well known, roti doesn't use any yeast and isn't served with a stuffing. It's super
easy to make and is a great addition to most meals!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 1/4 cup water

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, add the flour and drizzle a small amount of oil, about a teaspoons worth
  2. Slowly add the water until things start to come together.
    • You may not need the whole 1/4 cup of water, or you may need more. The important part is the consistency.
  3. Knead the mixture, adding flour or water as needed, until it no longer sticks to anything
  4. Portion off a little ball and roll it in your palms to form a little ball
  5. Throw some flour down and use a rolling pin to flatten it
    • You don't want it to be see-through, but it should be fairly thin
    • This is a good time to experiment with both portion size and thickness
  6. Once you've got it flat, throw it in a pan with medium heat until little air pockets form
  7. Flip it and let it cook until you get some nice brown spots on each side

This recipe takes some trial and error and you measure more with your heart than
anything else. Play around with portion size and how long you cook things until you find
a method that's perfect for you!
