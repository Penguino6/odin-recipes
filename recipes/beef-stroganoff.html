Beef Strombanoli

Description

Beef "Strombanoli" is just my household's version of beef stroganoff. I kept forgetting what it
was actually called so I would just make up a word when talking to my partner, and "strombanoli" is
what stuck. I play pretty fast and loose with the recipe, but it's basically just beef, mushrooms,
and noodles in a yummy sauce. We love mushrooms and don't care all that much for meat, so this recipe
is pretty fungi heavy.

Ingredients

  • olive oil
  • 1/2 stick of butter
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 shallot, diced
  • 2 cups portobello mushrooms
  • 1/4 beef, sliced to preferred size
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced
    • or however much you like, I always use more
  • 2 cups of preferred pasta shape
  • 1/3 cup parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1 small jar of alfredo sauce

Directions

  1. Using a high-walled saucepan, set your stove to medium heat.
  2. Once hot, add a drizzle of olive oil and a small chunk of butter
  3. Salt & pepper your beef, then add it to the pan and let it cook for about 5 minutes. You want a good sear on all sides
  4. Remove the beef and set it aside, leaving as much of the oil in the pan as possible
  5. Reduce the heat slightly and add in your onion and shallots
  6. Cover the onion/shallot mix with the mushrooms and let everything cook down
  7. After about 10 minutes, add salt & pepper, as well as any other seasonings you enjoy
  8. Mix things up every few minutes to let things cook evenly
  9. Once things have gotten soft and yummy smelling, add the water and the pasta
    • Cooking the pasta in with everything makes the noodles yummier!
  10. Once the noodles are cooked, things should be looking thicker. Add the alfredo sauce until you get your preferred sauce-to-food ration.
  11. Add the beef back in right after the sauce to get warm again, along with any juices that may be in the bowl.
  12. As for the parmesan, you can add some to the pan, or just sprinkle some on top of a serving taylor it an individuals preference
